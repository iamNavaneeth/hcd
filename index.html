2. Design Principles

#1 Balance

Balance is the distribution of the visual weight of objects, colors, texture, and space.

#2 Emphasis

Emphasis is the part of the design that catches the viewer’s attention.

#3 Movement

Movement is the path the viewer’s eye takes through the design.

#4 Unity / Consistency

Unity is the feeling of harmony between all parts of the design, which creates a sense of completeness.

Helps build the trust.

#5 Repetition

The repetition of elements of design creates unity within the interface.

#6 Proportion

Proportion is the feeling of unity created when all parts (sizes, amounts, or number) relate well with each other.

3. Layout and Composition

Building Blocks of the Interface Design.

They give the UI element a structure and make it easier to navigate.

There are few parameters to keep in mind!

1. Proximity

Objects that appear close to each other on a screen are related to each other

2. White space

Right amount of whitespace between the UI elements can help highlight important content

3. Alignment

Creates sharper, more visually-appealing design

4. Contrast

Contrast simply means that one item is different from another.

In layout and composition, contrast can help you do many things, like catch the reader's eye, create emphasis, or call attention to something important.

5. Experience Design

What's the difference between UX and XD?

UX - User Experience

focuses on “enhancing user satisfaction with a product by improving the usability, accessibility, and pleasure provided in the interaction with the product.”

XD - Experience Design

follows a broader mandate. It focuses on “moments of engagement, or touch-points, between people and brands, and the ideas, emotions, and memories that these moments create.”
